Self-Evaluation Examples for Software Engineers

Updated June 2026 · By the RISN team

Performance review season puts software engineers in a tricky spot: undersell yourself and you're overlooked, oversell and you lose credibility. Here's how software engineers should write a self-evaluation that lands.

How Software Engineers should structure a self-evaluation

Strong self-evaluations from software engineers follow a formula: specific accomplishments with quantified impact, evidence of operating above your level, and one genuine growth area with a plan. Every claim should answer 'what did I do, and what changed because of it?'

Accomplishment examples for Software Engineers

Whatever software engineers do, the key is translating activity into outcome. Don't write 'responsible for X' — write what X achieved, with a number attached wherever possible. Specificity is what gets remembered in the room where decisions are made.

What Software Engineers should avoid

Avoid vague superlatives like 'consistently exceeded expectations' with no evidence. Software Engineers who pair every strength with a specific example come across as confident; those who rely on adjectives come across as inflated.

How the ATS Resume Scanner Works

When you apply for a job online, your resume usually goes into an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) before any human sees it. The ATS scans your resume for keywords and phrases from the job description and ranks you partly on how well they match. Resumes with low keyword overlap often get filtered out automatically.

This free scanner does what the ATS does: it pulls the most important terms out of the job description and checks how many appear in your resume. The result is your match score and a list of the specific keywords you're missing — so you know exactly what to add.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good ATS match score?

Aim for 80% or higher. That means your resume covers most of the key terms the employer used. Between 60-80% is decent but improvable. Below 60% means you're likely missing important keywords and may be filtered out before a human sees your resume.

How do I improve my score?

Add the missing keywords the scanner identifies — using the exact phrasing from the job description wherever it's truthful for your experience. Mirror the employer's language rather than paraphrasing it. Include a dedicated skills section and use standard section headers like "Work Experience" and "Skills."
